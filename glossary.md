This glossary provides "Health Literacy" translations for complex clinical terms found throughout our research library.

## A
**Albumin:** A protein made by your liver. It helps keep fluid in your bloodstream so it doesn't leak into other tissues.

## C
**Creatinine:** A waste product in the blood that comes from muscle breakdown. It is used to estimate how well your kidneys are filtering (GFR).

**Cystatin C:** A protein produced by all cells in the body. Unlike creatinine, it is not affected by muscle mass, making it a more accurate marker for kidney function in some seniors.

## G
**GFR (Glomerular Filtration Rate):** The "Gold Standard" for measuring kidney function. It tells us how many milliliters of blood your kidneys filter every minute.

## S
**Sarcopenia:** The age-related loss of skeletal muscle mass and strength. This is why our "Protein Paradox" research is so critical.
