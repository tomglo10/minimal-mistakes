If you actually have Dengue, taking Ibuprofen can significantly <a href="https://www.aginghealth.website/2024/07/ shingles-vaccine-body-fat-dementia-risk.md" rel="dofollow" title="%20shingles Vaccine Body Fat Dementia Risk">**increase the risk**</a> of internal bleeding.</p>
###2026 Mosquito-Borne Disease FAQ
<p>**Q: Can I catch Dengue or Zika from a "winter" mosquito in the South?**</p><p>**A:** In 2026, the term "mosquito season" is becoming obsolete in the Deep South. While activity slows below **50°F**, urban heat islands and mild winters in cities like New Orleans, Houston, and Miami allow <i>Aedes</i> mosquitoes to survive year-round in storm drains and crawl spaces. If you have a fever and <a href="https://www.aginghealth.website/2025/12/january-thyroid-awareness-month.md" rel="dofollow" title="Empower Your Butterfly: January is Thyroid Awareness Month  | AGING HEALTH">**rash in January**</a>, do not rule out a mosquito-borne cause.</p>
<p>**Q: Why should I avoid Ibuprofen if I suspect a mosquito bite fever?**</p><p>**A:** This is a critical safety point. Many mosquito-borne illnesses, particularly **Dengue**, can <a href="https://www.aginghealth.website/2023/09/why-oxalates-could-cause-major-health.md" rel="dofollow" title="Oxalates - Why They May Cause Health Issues | AGING HEALTH">**cause your platelet**</a> count to drop. Ibuprofen (Advil/Motrin) and Aspirin are blood thinners that can increase the risk of internal bleeding. Until a <a href="https://www.aginghealth.website/2024/06/ent-doctor.md" rel="dofollow" title="Ent Doctor">**doctor confirms your diagnosis**</a>, stick exclusively to **Acetaminophen (Tylenol)** for <a href="https://www.aginghealth.website/2025/02/science-of-pain-causes-management.md" rel="dofollow" title="Science Of Pain Causes Management">**pain and fever**</a>.</p>
<p>**Q: Does "Oil of Lemon Eucalyptus" <a href="https://www.aginghealth.website/2024/08/what-is-crispr-how-does-it-work.md" rel="dofollow" title="What Is Crispr How Does It Work">**actually work as well**</a> as DEET?**</p><p>**A:** Yes, but with a catch. CDC-approved **OLE (or PMD)** is the only plant-based repellent that rivals DEET in efficacy. However, OLE typically requires more frequent reapplication-roughly every **2-3 hours** in high Southern humidity-whereas 25% <a href="https://www.aginghealth.website/2023/10/the-best-technology-advances-of-last.md" rel="dofollow" title="A New Age: Major Medical Advances in the Last 5 Years | AGING HEALTH">**DEET can last**</a> up to 8 hours.</p>
<p>**Q: If I've had Chikungunya once, can I get it again?** **A:** Current research suggests that a single <a href="https://www.aginghealth.website/2025/07/infection-vs-inflammation-guide.md" rel="dofollow" title="Infection Vs Inflammation Guide">**infection with Chikungunya**</a> likely provides **lifelong immunity**. However, you can still contract Dengue (which has four different strains) or Zika, as the antibodies do not cross-protect against different viruses.</p>
<p>**Q: Is "Microcephaly" still the only concern with Zika in 2026?**</p><p>**A:** While pregnancy remains the highest risk, 2026 data show a stronger correlation between Zika and **Guillain-Barré Syndrome (GBS)** in adults-a rare <a href="https://www.aginghealth.website/2024/07/mind-over-matter-brain-immune-system.md" rel="dofollow" title="Mind Over Matter: Brain's Control Over the Immune System | AGING HEALTH">**condition where the immune system attacks**</a> the nerves. If you experience tingling or weakness in your legs following a mosquito-related illness, <a href="https://www.aginghealth.website/2024/09/medical-debt.md" rel="dofollow" title="Medical Debt">**seek medical attention immediately**</a>.</p><div style="background-color: #fdfdfd; border-top: 3px solid rgb(0, 77, 64); color: #444444; font-size: 1.1rem; margin-top: 20px;"><button onclick="document.body.classList.toggle('audit-mode')">Toggle Sources
