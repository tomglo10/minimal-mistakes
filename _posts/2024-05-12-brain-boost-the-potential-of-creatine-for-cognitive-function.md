  <div class="ah-card">
    <h2 class="ah-title">Creatine 101: Your Brain's "Backup Battery"
    <p>The brain is an energy hog—consuming 20% of your body’s calories despite being only 2% of its weight. Creatine facilitates the rapid regeneration of **ATP (Adenosine Triphosphate)**, the universal energy currency of your cells.</p>
    <ul>
      <li>**Phosphocreatine (PCr):** This is the "stored" form of energy. When your brain faces a high-demand task (like learning a new skill), PCr donates a phosphate group to instantly create more ATP.</li>
      <li>**Cognitive Reserve:** By supplementing, we increase the brain's energy "buffer," allowing us to maintain focus longer without hitting a wall of mental fatigue.</li>
    </ul>
  </div>

  <div class="ah-update-box">
    <h3 class="ah-title" style="color: #1d4ed8;">🔬 March 2026 Clinical Synthesis: Creatine &amp; Aging
    <p>Current 2026 research highlights two specific scenarios where creatine is a "must-consider" for older adults:</p>
    <ul>
      <li>**The Sleep Deprivation Window:** A 2024 study in <em>Scientific Reports</em> proved that a single dose of creatine can "re-charge" the brain during periods of poor sleep, temporarily reversing cognitive decline in processing speed.</li>
      <li>**The Sarcopenia Connection:** Creatine is the perfect partner for our 2026 protein target of **1.2–1.5 g/kg**. It increases muscle cell hydration and supports the resistance training needed to prevent frailty.</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
